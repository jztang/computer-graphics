Final Project - Rubik's Cube

The goal of my final project was to create a working Rubik's Cube using ray tracing to half spaces. My
implementation involves 27 cube half spaces, each representing a part of a standard 3x3 Rubik's Cube.

Each "small" cube is labeled 1-27. Cubes 1-9 are the back layer, cubes 10-18 are the middle layer, and
cubes 19-27 are the front layer. I wasn't sure how to make "timed" animations such that the cube
appeared to solve itself, or how to make different faces of the same ray traced cube have different
colors. So I just have the faces rotating constantly, and cube half spaces in one color.

When I tried to render the cube, my PC's CPU usage shot up to 100%. I guess ray tracing to 27 different
half spaces is very resource intensive. I have the Rubik's Cube rotating, but the animation is very choppy
because of the high CPU usage. So click the examples below at your own risk, as your computer will
probably slow down a lot when it runs.
